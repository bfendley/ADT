Application steps

We review scholarship and grant applications on a rolling basis. Set yourself up for success by sharing clear work samples and showing how your project supports Arkansas communities.

  1. Prepare a concise summary of your project, the impact so far, and what support will help you achieve.
  2. Collect links or files that demonstrate your work, such as code repositories, prototypes, or presentation decks.
  3. Download the scholarship or grant form, fill out, and email to funding@ardigitaltrailblazers.com

Scholarships

Support for students and independent learners who can show what they've built and how they plan to grow Arkansas' talent pipeline.

ADT Scholarship — Monticello High School

Awarded to Monticello High School seniors pursuing technology, automation, data, or software pathways. Share a project you built (or are building) and how it helped you learn or serve others.

ADT Scholarship — Non-Traditional Learners

A brief, flexible scholarship for Arkansans changing careers or returning to school. Show your momentum with a small artifact—an automation, script, dashboard, write-up, or prototype—and what the award will unlock next.

Download Scholarship Form (PDF)

What we look for

  • A project or artifact you built: code, automation, dashboard, documentation, or research.
  • A short story of the problem you tackled, your approach, the impact, and what you learned.
  • A plan for how you'll share knowledge or mentor others in the community.

What you receive

  • $1,000 award with an optional $250 equipment stipend.
  • Mentorship, resume and portfolio feedback, plus a mock interview.
  • Spotlight feature in the Trailblazer Legends series.

Funds can be used for tuition, certification fees, hardware, travel, or launch costs. Academy participants needing fee waivers can indicate that in the application.

Grants

Grants for projects that prototype, ship, or share something useful with Arkansas—whether in classrooms, communities, or small teams.

Trailblazer Micro-Grants

Flexible funding that meets your project where it is. Pick the track that aligns with your next milestone.

  • Idea Validation: $250–$400 for domains, prototyping tools, user interviews, or early research.
  • Build Sprint: $500–$750 to ship an MVP, automation, dataset, or workflow that serves an Arkansas team.
  • Community Impact: $500–$1,000 to host events, workshops, or open-source contributions that grow local talent.

Download Grant Form (PDF)

All micro-grant recipients share a short public update and schedule a 30-minute knowledge share with the community.
